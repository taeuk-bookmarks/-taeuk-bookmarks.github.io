I've seen some isolated instances of people putting expires: YYYY-MM-DD in the frontmatter of Jekyll posts, but my initial tests have proved fruitless.

Is there a way to automatically expire posts, that is, prevent building a post after a specified date, similar to the published: false flag? If not, can this be implemented, pretty please?

parkr commented on 7 Jul 2014

Utilize site.time:

{% assign curr_date = site.time | date: "%Y-%m-%d" %}
{% if curr_date < page.expires %}
    {{ page.title }}
{% endif %}

troyswanson commented on 9 Jul 2014

⚠️ Warning ⚠️

Please be aware that an expired post will not automatically disappear; you will have to rebuild the site after a post expires. This is due to the static nature of Jekyll.

If you build the site before a post expires, the logic will display the post. Once the post expires, nothing will change until you rebuild the site again and the logic decides to hide the post.
